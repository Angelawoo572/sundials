Hello Kai,

CVODE's choice of stepsize to be attempted on the first step depends
in a mild way on the initial 'log interval' |tout - t0|.  Generally,
this results in at most a slight variation in run statistics as a
function of the tout sequence.  But in your case, there seems to be
some difficulty taking the first step, related to a time constant in
your system on the order of 10^{-45}.  That would indicate either a
bug (perhaps a bad Jacobian), or a genuine very short initial time
scale in your system (which may or may not correspond to a fast
transient in the solution).  In the latter case, we would urge you to
use a log interval that is much nearer to that time scale, so that a
more suitable initial stepsize is chosen (and the reported solution
values resolve that transient).

-Alan Hindmarsh
