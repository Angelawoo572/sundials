Herbert,

My solution to this problem was to replace all printfs and fprintfs in 
CVODE with my own defined MY_FPRINTF(...) [which can be done with a 
simple global replace]. Then, I handle the strings coming into this 
function my own way and combine it into my logging facility (which is 
nice since CVODE has been incorporated as a library and is a small piece 
of my code). This way, log messages from many different libraries can be 
aggregated and piped to the same place. You also have the advantage of 
thread safety since you control the internals of MY_PRINTF and your 
logging facility.

Emery
