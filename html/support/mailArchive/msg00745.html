> > points y_{n-i}. I can not find the point where \dot y_{n-1} is plugged in.
> 
> What you need to realize is that the both the predictor and corrector 
> polynomials are encoded in the Nordsieck history array. As such, at the 
> beginning of a new step, the Nordsieck array encodes the corrector polynomial
> 
> from the previous step which becomes the predictor polynomial for the current
> 
> step.  In other words, suppose we've just successfully reached time t_{n-1}.
> 
> This means that the Nordsieck array has just been corrected to contain the 
> corrector polynomial at that step which satisfies \dot y_{n-1} = \dot
> \omega_{c, 
> n-1} (t_{n-1}) (because this is how we actually define the BDF on the
> previous 
> step - see step (3) in my previous email).  But \omega_{c,n-1} \equiv 
> \omega_{p,n} and therefore \dot \omega_{p,n} (t_{n-i}) = \dot y_{n-1}.
>
