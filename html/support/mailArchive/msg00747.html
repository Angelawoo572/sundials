Yes. But [] means activity of each species as opposed to plain mol number or concentration (which unfortunately doesn't allow a calculation as you proposed).

If the reaction is reversible, does the amount of NaCl matter?

No. The activity of solids are defined to be always 1. Hence the solution only depends on the activities of the ions Na+ and Cl- and of course the mass balance, where all species need to be considered. The activities are non-linear functions of the mol numbers of the ions (or molalities).

Assuming my simplified equilibrium equation is correct and using the conservation equations, then z should be solution of

K = (x_0 - z)(y_0 - z)/z  <=>
z^2 - (K+x_0+y_0)*z + x_0*y_0 = 0

and this quadratic equation always has real solutions for x_0, y_0, K > What am I missing? :-)

Unfortunately I cannot simply use your version of simplified equilibrium equation :-(

Just to give you an example, here are some equlibrium states for this system (at some temperature around 10 degrees Celsius):

1 Na+   1 Cl-  0 NaCl
2 Na+   2 Cl-  0 NaCl
3 Na+   3 Cl-  0 NaCl
4 Na+   4 Cl-  0 NaCl
5 Na+   5 Cl-  0 NaCl
6 Na+   6 Cl-  0 NaCl  <- Solution becomes saturated with respect to NaCl
6 Na+   6 Cl-  1 NaCl
6 Na+   6 Cl-  2 NaCl
6 Na+   6 Cl-  3 NaCl

Only the last 4 equations can be solved correctly with Kinsol (or alternative iteration methods).

For this small system it seems trivial, but in my real system I have 6 different ions and 20 different salts and hydrate phases :-(
