Re: does a sparse NVECTOR implementation make sense?

From Alan Hindmarsh <alanh@llnl.gov>
Date Wed, 24 Aug 2005 13:25:32 -0700 (PDT) 
Andrey-

Are you on a serial machine?  If so, you might want to look at LSODES
or VODE-F90, which have sparse treatments of the Jacobian.  They are
Fortran solvers, and do not have the sensitivity extensions, but maybe
you can still use the sparse linear algebra parts.  LSODES uses YSMP
and VODE-F90 uses MA28, so you have a choice.

-Alan H.
