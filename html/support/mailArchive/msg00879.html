Hi all

I am working on using the IDA solver to tackle a simplified 1-D
two-phase flow problem. I've been able to produce a *small* stable model
(about 50 nodes) and solve it using IDA with mostly the default parameters.

However I'm having trouble increasing the number of nodes in my model,
and also with increasing the total flow-path length of my pipe.

This gets into stability of BDF methods when applied to PDAE systems. I
have tried to find some good references on this topic but haven't been
able to find much. I have the Ascher and Petzold book about DAE systems,
and I have the Patankar book /Numerical Heat Transfer and Fluid Flow/.
I've also read various old and new papers including a good one by Carver
and Hinds (1978) from /Simulation/. The Petzold paper about 'DAEs are
not ODEs' was good too.

I found that specialist solvers for my type of problem do exist (eg a
French one called CATHARE) but I would like to understand the stability
problems so that I can use a general solver, preferably IDA.

Can anyone suggest some good reading about stability with implicit PDAE
systems? How do Courant stability criteria translate to the case of
implicit BDF integrators? Are there any 'gotchas' in solving
convection/two-phase problems with fully implicit solvers that are well
known?

Any suggestions would be much appreciated.

Cheers
