the SBaddon package uses CVODE. The difference to your approach
is that the model is converted to C, compiled and linked with the CVODES package.

So you get good performance while still using MATLAB and
you do not even have to care about any compilation, linux, cygwin, etc.

Its plug & play :)

Ning Jiang wrote:

Hi, guys,

I am very new to SUNDIALS, so my questions may seem naïve. I am an engineering guy, and do not have much experiences on the performances of numerical calculations. Basically, I am now working on neuron model (modified Hodgkin-Huxley model), which involves ODE system with anywhere from 2 to over 1000 equations. I am mainly using Matlab 7, which works fine except for its speed. Some guy told me that SUNDIALS could dramatically improve the calculation performance.

After installing sundialTB, I did the following:

1, I wrote my RHS function:

function [yd,flag,new_data]=cvode_hh(t,y,data)

yd(1) = -(data.gl*(y(1)-data.vl)+data.gna/(1+exp(-(y(1)+data.thetam)/data.sm))^3*(1-y(2))*(y(1)-data.vna)+data.gk*y(2)^4*(y(1)-data.vk))+data.inp;

yd(2) =data.phi*(1/(1+exp((y(1)-data.thetan)/data.sn))-y(2))/(data.taun0+data.taun1/(1+exp((y(1)-data.thn)/data.sigman)));

flag = 0;

new_data = [];

2, the options that I used is:

options = CVodeSetOptions('RelTol',1.e-8,...

'AbsTol',[1.e-8; 1.e-14],...

'LinearSolver','Dense');

3, memory allocation:

CVodeMalloc(@cvode_hh,t0,y0,options,neurons);

Where “neurons” is the structure of parameters.

I used fixed time steps.

It turned out that there is merely no speed improvement (on a 2-equation system) compared with Matlab. I am wondering if there is anything that I can do to make the most out of SUNDAILS’ capabilities.

Thank you very much for any suggestions and recommendations!
