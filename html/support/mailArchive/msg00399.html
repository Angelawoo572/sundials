Andreas Nicolai wrote: 
Hi there,

I have an ODE system of the following form:

C_uu du/dt + C_uv dv/dt = rhs_u
C_vu du/dt + C_vv dv/dt = rhs_v

The unknowns are u and v and rhs stands for "right hand side". Can this system of differential equations be solved with CVODE or which other solver from SUNDIALS would be suitable?

Bye,
Andreas


Hi Andreas,

CVODE and CVODES are designed to solve ODEs in the so-called "explicit form", that is y' = f(t,y).
For a system of the form A(y)y' = f(t,y), such as yours, you can either try to convert it to the above form (if the inversion of A is trivial, which I doubt is the case for your problem) and continue using CVODE, or else switch to IDA which is designed to deal with the even more general form F(t,y',y)=0.
For IDA, instead of the RHS function, you'll have to provide a residual function, i.e. a function that returns A(y)y'-f(t,y).

--Radu
