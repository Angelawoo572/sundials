Hello Anatoliy,

The algorithm for selection of stepsizes and orders in CVODE/CVODES is
based on estimated local errors, and is rather complex.  There is no
easy way to explain why the choices you see are what they are.  All
I can say is that in general, as you reduce tolerances, you tend to
see higher orders.  You may want to try reducing tolerances even more
and see what orders are chosen.  Also, if you are running in normal
mode, you are not seeing order values from all steps, only those
from the last step to cross tout in each call.  If you add more tout
values, or use one-step mode, you will see more values.

-Alan H
