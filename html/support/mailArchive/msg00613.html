By this I assume you mean CVODES with the nonstiff option (Adams/Functional).
If you are not doing sensitivity calculations, I suggest switching to
CVODE for this, just because there is less code to keep around.

>    I am just thinking about put it into parallel version to make
> the problem scalable (now I only have 2000 state variable). And
> I am exploring whether that is some efficient methods for my problem.
>

Parallelization of your problem in this case is fairly simple.  I assume
the problem form is either A y' = f(t,y) or A y" = f(t,y,y').  There
are the following two jobs.  (Since there is no SUNDIALS linear solver
being used, things are much simpler than they would be otherwise.)
(1) Generate f in parallel.  This just means that when a given processor
requires components of y [or y' in the f(t,y,y') case] that reside on a
different processor, appropriate communication operatins must be done at
the start of your right-hand side evaluation function.
(2) Generate A^{-1} f in parallel.  If A is a band matrix with no other
particular structure to exploit, you probably need to get a parallel
band matrix solver. The ScaLAPACK collection has one.
