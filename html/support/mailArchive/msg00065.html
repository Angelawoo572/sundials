Yes, as we (try to) stress in the documentation, a user-supplied
Jacobian need not be exact, and need not even have the same bandwidth
as the true J if a narrower approximation is good.  As you say, the
Newton matrix (M = I - c J) only has to steer the corrections into
roughly the right values (it needs to do better than just the right
direction, though).  So you need to determine what is most significant
numerically in J, and supply that.  It would be nice of the expensive
terms from splines etc. are not numerically sizable and so can be
ignored, but you may not be so lucky.  It may end up being easier to
try alterations to CVBandDQJac.

Here's another thing you should definitely try.  Choose SPGMR as the
linear solver, and use a banded preconditioner.  The CVBANDPRE module
lets you do that with a difference quotient J (again).  But if/when
you get an analytic approximation written, you can try that directly
(with CVSpgmrPreconditioner).  The point here is that this allows
GMRES iteration to correct for errors in the Jacobian, with no extra
setup effort.  Very often the combination (approx. J + GMRES) works
much better than either one alone.

-Alan
