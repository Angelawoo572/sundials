> Hi,
> 
> I eventually got around incorporating CVODE in my Delphi 
> application. It's working very well. Basically my approach 
> has been as follows:
> 
> - Compile the original C code using the free Borland C++ 5.5 
> compiler (see
> http://www.borland.com/downloads/download_cbuilder.html)
> - Link the object files into a Delphi unit that gives me 
> access to all the procedures/functions I am interested in, as 
> well to some structures (in particular N_Vector).
> 
> That works like a gem, even though I don't have all of the 
> CVODE functionalities. Indeed, at this stage, I can 'only' 
> use the Adams-Moulton and BDF solvers with a functional 
> iterator. Still, it works fine and doesn't require any DLL or 
> anything.
> 
> On a side note, I remember reading on this mailing list 
> something about Herbert Sauro (Jarnac) saying that LSODA was 
> much slower than CVODE. I would, in fact, expect LSODA to be 
> better, since it can switch between Adams-Moulton and BDF, 
> depending on the stifness of the problem, right?
> 
> Still, besides incorporating CVODE in my application, I also 
> incorporated Herbert's Delphi version of LSODA and like him, 
> I noticed that LSODA was much slower (sometimes even 10 times 
> slower, depending on the problem I am solving). I don't think 
> LSODA itself is to be blamed, but maybe its implementation in 
> Delphi and, more probably in fact, Delphi itself. Indeed, the 
> CVODE version that I have has been compiled in an optimised 
> way (or as optimised as it can be using the Borland C++ 5.5 
> compiler and as far as I can tell), while Delphi is, 
> unfortunately, known for being rather bad at optimising code. 
> That, I believe, could be the reason as to why the Delphi 
> version of LSODA compares rather unfavourably against CVODE.
> 
> I will (in a month or so, as I don't think I will have time 
> before then) try to use a C version of LSODA that I have 
> found on the internet 
> (http://server.ccl.net/cca/software/SOURCES/C/kinetics2/lsoda-dir/).
> Incidently, has anyone tried to use this version? Or, does 
> anyone know of a good C version of LSODA?
> 
>       Cheers, Alan.
