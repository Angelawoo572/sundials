> In fact, the dense linear solver requires storage for two 
> n-by-n dense matrices (for the Jacobian and a saved copy of 
> the Jacobian) plus an n-dimensional array for the pivots. 
> That's about 900Mb.
> 
> The dense linear solver is probably the worst choice for such 
> problems as it does not take into account structure and/or 
> sparsity of the Jacobian.
> 
> Depending on how you semi-discretize your PDE, a banded 
> linear solver may be appropriate. This one requires two 
> banded matrices plus an array of pivots (see section 10.2 in 
> the user guide for the storage requirements for a banded matrix).
> 
> However, a better choice is, as Andreas suggested, using one 
> of the iterative linear solvers which are matrix-free and 
> only require storage for a few vectors of dimension n (for 
> the Krylov basis vectors and some temporary space). Of 
> course, in this case, you'll have to think about an 
> appropriate preconditioner.
> 
> Finally, you may consider a sparse direct linear solver. At 
> this time we do not provide one within SUNDIALS, but you 
> could either write the appropriate interfaces for CVODE or, 
> as a quicker way to hook up a new linear solver to CVODE, use 
> one of the iterative linear solvers and make the sparse 
> direct linear solver your preconditioner.

Ok, it would seem that I will have to dig into the documentation, as I have

it would seem that it's worth investigating, so I will definitely give it a
