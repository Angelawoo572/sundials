Hello Xuedong,

In fact, CVODE and IDA do use information about y' (and y) at previous
times, quite intensively.  This is because they use multistep methods,
and carry a history of past values (in an appropriate transformed form).
This all happens internally.  It is not necessary (and is in fact harmful)
to involve any data at t-dt when writing user code for function values at t.
These methods are the subject of research that goes back about 100 years.
For general problems, with fairly smooth solutions, these multistep
methods are almost always more efficient than Runge-Kutta methods,
precisely because they use past history, while RK methods do not.

-Alan
