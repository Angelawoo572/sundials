It seems that in you CV_NORMAL code, tout is increasing. Hence after
CVodeReInit, you still have tout > tret (which becomes new initial
time), so still get positive steps. But in CV_ONE_STEP code, t_init is
fixed. At some stage, t_init+0.01 < tret, you will get a negative step.

Yi

>Jordan:
>A couple of points:
>1) To reinitialize CVODE, use CVodeReInit instead of CVodeMalloc, since

>the problem size remains the same. You do not need to reallocate memory

>for cvode_mem...
>2) After reinitialization, the integrator will restart from order 1. 
>Although we do provide a function to set the initial step size 
>(CVodeSetInitStep), keep in mind that the step size before the 
>reinitialization is based on whatever the current method order was at 
>that time and could therefore be too large. Better let CVODE select it.
>--Radu

>>>As far as I know, in order to do this you have to reinitialize the 
>>>solver.   The vector y returned by your call to CVode(mem, t, y, 
>>>&treturn, itask) is not the state vector internally maintained by the

>>>solver.   The solver may have proceeded past your integration time t,
and 
>>>is giving you an interpolation back to the time requested, so its 
>>>internal state may already be into the future.    You want to change
the 
>>>internal state of the solver, and this requires initialization.   You

>>>might want to get the current internal step size of the solver and 
>>>use that when you reinitialize.
