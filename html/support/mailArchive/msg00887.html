Hi Andreas,

I am afraid I am a little hesitant to use finite difference to calculate the jacobian, primarily due to CPU expense. I am solving a 3-D conduction heat transfer equation with temperature dependent material properties, nonlinear radiation BC, and a combined internal cavity radiation. And the combined problem is expensive to solve. Hence, if a jacobian is calculated using finite difference, the f() function will have to be calculated as many times as the number of dofs in the model.

Moreover, I already have the jacobian calculation in place for the f() function. So, I only need to add some extra computing for the jacobian of the restated problem.

Manav



On Feb 22, 2007, at 12:43 PM, Andreas Nicolai wrote:

Hi again,

let CVODE figure out the Jacobian for you using its default difference quotient approximation. Simply implement the f() function. For a simple heat transfer equation with some non-linear terms on the right-hand-side CVODE already performs extremely well. A user-defined Jacobian would only make sense of your right-hand- side terms are really non-linear over several orders of magnitude. But in my experience, even if you include some heavily non-linear radiation terms, this is not a problem for CVODE.

Andreas

PS: are you solving 1D or 2D or 3D problems?
