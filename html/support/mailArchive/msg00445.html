You can print the value of the Jacobian by calling DensePrint or BandPrint, 
depending on what linear solver you use.

Are there any limits on the number of sensitivities (sensitivity parameters)? Can the number of sensitivities be greater than the number of equations?
E.g., I have some dynamical problem, where the solution components (NEQ) depend on their initial conditions and some masses in the equations of motions. Here the number of sensitivities is greater, than the NEQ. 

There is no limit on the number of sensitivity parameters.

I have a segmentation fault when I try to set initial conditions in such a case. Here is a short code, where it is (NS>NEQ and y is N_Vector of NEQ dimension).
yS=N_VCloneVectorArray_Serial(NS, y);
if(check_flag((void *)yS, "N_VCloneVectorArray_Serial", 0)) return(1);
for(i=0;i<NS;i++){
N_VConst(ZERO, yS[i]);
if(i%7-6) Ith(yS[i],i)=ONE;
printf("%lf\n", Ith(yS[i],i));}
The allocation gives no errors. But when I tried to print assigned values out, it gave me the lesser list of values and segmentation fault. Is it a problem of my compilation?

I assume you use the macro Ith as defined in the CVODES examples. If so, notice that its second argument runs from 1 and not 0. When i=0, you are accessing memory outside that allocated for your vectors, hence the SEGFAULT. Use Ith(yS[i],i+1).
