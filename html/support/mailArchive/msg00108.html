> As noted in the documentation, each nonlinear iteration 
> method is better suited for different types of problems: 
> functional iteration for nonstiff problems, Newton iteration 
> for stiff problems. It is therefore very easy to come up with 
> problems for which one will perform much better than the other.
> 
> Consider the ODE y'=lambda*y (the famous Dahlquist test 
> problem) with some large negative lambda and see for yourself 
> what happens for the two different nonlinear solvers 
> (functional vs. Newton) if you take a backward Euler step. 
> That should give you a hint on how to construct a simple 
> example for which functional iteration will perform much 
> better than Newton :-)
> 
> What was the ODE you tested?

Various systems of 15+ ODEs. They are stiff systems describing the
electrical activity of cardiac cells.
