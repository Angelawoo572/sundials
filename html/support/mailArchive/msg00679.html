You are right, Alan. A simple example of such system is a jacketed continuous 
stirred tank reactor (CSTR) in which a first order exothermic reaction takes 
place. The plot showing generated (QG) and removed (QR) heat as a function of 
reactor temperature may have the following look:

^  QR(,), QG(*)
|
|                                     ,  QR
|                                   ,
|                               3 ,
|                            *  ,*   *   *   * QG
|                        *    ,
|                      *    ,
|                     *   ,
|                    *  ,
|                   * ,
|                   ,
|                 ,   2
|               ,  *
|             ,
|           ,    *
|         ,    *
|*   *  ,*  *
|     ,  1
|   ,
|--------------------------------------------------->
                                              TR

One can see that the there are three intersection and each of them is a steady 
state. It is noteworthy that the upper and the lower temperature steady 
states (1 and 3) are "stable", and the middle one (2) is an "unstable" 
equilibrium point. So, if one tries to integrate this ODE system with an 
initial point between (1) and (2),  the steady state result will be (1). If 
the initial state is between (2) and (3) the system will converge to (3).

Hope this helps.
