Andreas Nicolai wrote: 
Hi,

this is a "harmless" error. Just call CVODE again. Alternatively set the mxstep parameter to a really large number. If you then end up with huge calculation times, something could be wrong with the ODE system. In this case monitor your CVODE status variables, mainly the fail-counts. If you get lots of convergence failures, it indicates that your model/implementation has a problem...

Andreas


Andreas is correct. CVODE issues this error (you may want to think of it more as a warning) precisely because, more often than not, too many steps indicate a problem with the ODE and/or the settings you use for the numerical solution. If this is the same ODE you sent me yesterday, I would strongly recommend you take another look at the tolerances used (give some thought to what absolute tolerances are appropriate for each solution component).
