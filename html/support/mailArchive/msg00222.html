Hi Andreas,

You should not attempt to modify the variables integrated by CVODE outside the solver.
I see two options for you:
a) lag the two sets of variables in time (search for "time splitting", "operator splitting", "fractional step" methods)
b) solve the whole coupled system with CVODE and, for efficiency, ignore the nonstiff variables and equations in your Jacobian approximation functions.

--Radu

Andreas Nicolai wrote: 
Hi there,

I have a question about CVODE. In order to reduce the size of my system, I would like to integrate some variables outside the main integration loop of CVODE. For example, I have 3 highly coupled and strongly non-linear quantities that I integrate with CVODE. But then I have 5 other quantities that influence the system rather marginally, but depend on the solution of the first 3 (primary) quantities. Also the accuracy requirements for these other 5 variables are much lower.

Also, for these other 5 quantities numerical stability isn't critical, so I would now simply use CVODE in its one-step-mode and use an explicit scheme to integrate those 5 variables at the end of each time step. This works fine and very fast, too.

But...

Some of these 5 variables require now changes of one of my primary variables. Obviously, I may not simply change the variables that I use as input for CVODE (in each new call of the CVODE integrator). At least when I tried to do so, I got weird results. I believe that doing so I would introduce an unsteadyness in the solution and the whole history of past time steps and extrapolation goes down the drain.

-----
Here is a short example to clarify the problem:

Primary quantities are: Temperature and salt concentration
Secondary quantities (not integrated with CVODE) are: solid salt mass concentration

After each step of CVODE I calculate the approximate production rate for my salt and calculate the change of mass for my solid salt. But I also have to remove the salt mass from the solution and change the concentration. However, salt concentration is an integrated quantity in CVODE and I cannot do that simply in between calls to CVODE.
-----

Now, is there a safe/recommanded way to modify my solution variables after each step that CVODE took without the need to reinitialize the solver every time?

One "hack" I could think of is to "remember" the change I want to make to the variable and simply add it as source/sink term when I next calculate my divergences inside my f() function during the next CVODE integration.


Thanks for any suggestions,
Andreas
