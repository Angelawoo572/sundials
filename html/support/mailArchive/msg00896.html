Hi Andreas,

I have one additional comment in answer to your first question, about
the direct solution of Jacobians from 3D problems.

For an explicit ODE system obtained from a 3D advection-diffusion PDE
treated by nearest-neighbor differences, the coupling in the ODE
Jacobian is either 7-point coupling (at a minimum) or something more
involved, up to 27-point coupling.  The standard banded difference
quotient scheme uses a number of RHS function evaluations equal to the
bandwidth, which goes as the product of two grid dimensions.  As you
say, this can be a very big number.  Instead, you can use a difference
quotient scheme based on 27-point coupling that uses exactly 27
RHS evaluations, independent of grid dimensions.  If you have only
7-point coupling, this may sound too expensive, but I suspect it
is in fact optimal or very close to it, and it certainly beats the
band algorithm.

For a system of p PDEs in 3D of advection-diffusion-reaction form,
you can get DQ Jacobians at a cost of 27p RHS evaluations, by an
extension of the above scheme.

-Alan H
