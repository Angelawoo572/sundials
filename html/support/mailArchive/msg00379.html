deep temporary copy of the data if you need to modify it in your other function. However, if modification of your solver variables is what you want, try the following:

1. create copy of calculated y values after completing a time step
2. call other func and modify temporary variables
3. calculate differences between modified and original variables
4. add those differences as constant sources/sinks to the next time step

I know this is a hack, but if you solution isn't too sensitive, this allows a very simple decoupling of the CVODE integration and an external modification/adjustment of the y variables.

This is the wrong approach for at least two reasons:
1) You would merely replace a discontinuity in the solution with one in the RHS (i.e. in y') and, as I pointed out in my long post on this subject, both types of discontinuities REQUIRE a restart of the integrator.
2) Moreover, if Raj actually wants a sharp jump in y at such a discontinuity point, you could not capture that with an additional forcing term that attempts to drive the solution towards the desired new value.
