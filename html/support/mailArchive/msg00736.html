Hello Radu,

I have a question concerning the corrector equation. In the following I use the
same symbols as in your paper "The PVODE and IDA Algorithms" by Alan Hindmarsh.
 
For a fixed-leading coefficient implementation of BDF equation the predictor
polynomial interpolates (Newton) the calculated y values (past grid values)
taking into account, that the step width varies. That means, the y values used
to determine the predictor polynomial coeficients can be non-equidistant.
In contrast, the corrector polynomial is based on equidistant y values, which
are calculated from the past grid values. The corrector equation is said to be:

alpha_s(y_n-y_n(0))+h_n(y_n_dot-y_n(0)_dot).

The derivation of the above equation is what I am interested in. What is
normally done to explain the derivation is, that for the corrector polynomial
omega_c and the predictor poynomial omega_p the following approach is made:

omega_c(t)=omega_p(t)+c(t)(y_n-y_n(0)).

Next this equation is derived and "evaluated for tn". Can you please explain me
this "evaluation for tn". How do I come from c_dot(tn) to 1/(h_n*Beta_0)?

Closely linked with this problem is another (very small) question. For the
predictor polynomial it is assumed that omega_p_dot(t_(n-1))=y_dot_(n-1). I
wonder how this is fulfilled by the Newton interploation done in the above
mentioned paper.

Could you please help me?

Thank you very much in advance.
