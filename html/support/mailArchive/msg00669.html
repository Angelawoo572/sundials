There may indeed not be a significantly faster way to get a steady state
than to integrate until the solution stops changing.  You can try
loosening tolerances, but this is risky -- the integrator may fail.
The problem is that there may be more than one steady state, and an
inaccurate integration (or other approach) may find one that is
different from what you get by accurate integration, and so is
somehow inconsistent with your initial conditions, and hence probably
not the one you want.

-Alan H.
