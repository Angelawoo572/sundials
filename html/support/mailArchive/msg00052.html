Hello Herbert,

Your comment prompts me to ask: Do you know what is making CVODE run
much faster than LSODA?

LSODA has considerable extra overhead for detecting stiffness and
switching methods accordingly.  Another Fortran solver would solve
your problem just as well, but without that overhead, given the
correct method choice.  Depending on what CVODE method you are
choosing, you might what to try LSODE or VODE (for the direct linear
solver case) or LSODPK or VODPK (for the Krylov solver case), to see
if this overhead is the reason.

Alternatively, if you are seeing a speedup just by virtue of the
change from Fortran to C, that would be useful to know too.

Thanks for any info you provide on this.

-Alan Hindmarsh
