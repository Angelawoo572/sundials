Hi there,

while trying to understand the process of error control for the time integration, I found 
a line in the documentation of CVODE saying that "We allow at most 3 iterations (but 
this limit can be changed by the user)." right after introducing the convergence 
criterion.

Now, before discussing this further, I have a general question about how CVODE controls 
the error in time. For my previous hand-written 1st order implicit Euler scheme, I found 
the following behaviour: As long as the solution did not diverge, at some point (even 
after many iterations) and sometimes for a time step that was much too large at this 
step, the solution converged and I could continue with integration in time (thanks to the 
unconditionally stable implicit scheme). However, when choosing too large time steps, the 
errors in the solution were also pretty big (sometimes even "jumping" and 
ignoring important effects in the solution).

My solution was to limit the number of allowed iterations, and with a Newton 
scheme I had typically a limit of 10 iterations until convergence - if more 
were needed, I had to reduce my time step and try again.

Is that in principle the same procedure as used in CVODE? If the number of 
iterations is bigger than a certain limit, the time step must be too large and 
is reduced? Or is there a different reason for the iteration limit?

Also, does this "arbitrary" iteration limit actually guarantee that the error 
term for the time discretization stays below a certain value or is this also controled 
elsewhere?
