Dear CW,

You should not use "pwr3" since your system is based on the PowerPC architecture and not on the POWER hardware platform. You could try the following:

 $ CC=xlc F77=xlf --with-cflags="-qarch=auto -qtune=auto" \
   --with-fflags="-qarch=auto -qtune=auto" \
   --with-mpicc=mpcc \
   --with-mpif77=mpxlf

--Aaron

But I think I've pretty much nailed down the source of the problem, pending your approval. After I changed the compilers to gcc and gcc, I was able to compile the serial codes to get the serial library files and create the serial examples. But I couldn't do that with the parallel codes because the parallel codes are compiled with the "mpicc" and "mpif77" scripts, which use "xlc" and "xlf" (IBM c/fortran compilers, scripts attached) to compile the codes. Hence I get error messages in the when I "make" and "make examples".

I wonder if there's some incompatibility between Sundials and xlc/xlf? If my assessment of the problem is wrong, what would be your take?

CW,

A quick Google search will show that there are several incompatibilities between gcc and xlc, which is apparently what happens in your case (look at the compilation errors for nvec_par in your Sundials_make.txt file. Most of the subsequent errors you see in compiling the parallel examples come from the fact that nvec_par was not installed!).

That being said, there is no incompatibility between SUNDIALS and xlc/xlf. SUNDIALS has been compiled on AIX without problems. however, we do not mix gcc and xlc.

Is there any reason why you do not use CC=xlc and F77=xlf? FYI, here's a set of configure options that we used to build SUNDIALS with xlc/xlf. Try them out:

CC=xlc F77=xlf --with-cflags="-qarch=pwr3 -qtune=pwr3 -qmaxmem=8192" --with-fflags="-qarch=pwr3 -qtune=pwr3 -qmaxmem=8192" --with-mpicc=mpcc --with-mpif77=mpxlf

--Radu
